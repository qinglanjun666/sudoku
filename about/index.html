About Me

Who I Am

I am the creator of Logic for Sudoku, a Sudoku enthusiast who values pure logic, reusable methods, and clear explanations. This site exists to shift Sudoku from “luck” back to “reasoning”, helping you progress from beginner to expert and systematically master each technique.

Site Focus

Logic for Sudoku is a logic‑first tutorial site whose core goal is solving without guessing. Content is structured by skill levels: from basic rules and candidate notation, to fish, wings, and chaining; then uniqueness techniques and complex set‑based methods, ultimately covering advanced techniques and meta‑logic.

What You’ll Get Here

  • Clear concept explanations: terminology, structures, constraints, and recognition cues.
  • Standard reasoning frameworks: strong/weak links, AIC, loops, geometric and set thinking.
  • Transferable practice methods: observation, notation, patterns, and short‑chain training.
  • Strategies to avoid trial‑and‑error: stick to short chains and visual reasoning; no trial‑fill and backtracking.

Why “Logic for Sudoku”

The name states the philosophy: Sudoku is not arithmetic; it is constraint satisfaction and logical elimination. Every chapter revolves around verifiable logical steps, helping you find reliable breakthroughs at any level of difficulty.

Vision

Make complexity simple, turn techniques into methods, and methods into intuition. Whether you are a beginner, intermediate, or advanced solver, you should be able to complete elegant solutions through clear logic.
