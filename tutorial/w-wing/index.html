W-Wing

1. What You Will Master

In this section, you will learn the W-Wing. Unlike the XY-Wing (which uses three cells: XY, XZ, YZ), the W-Wing uses two identical cells (e.g., pair AB and pair AB) that are connected by a Strong Link. You will learn how to use a single-digit pattern to force a relationship between two bi-value cells, leading to a guaranteed elimination.

2. Main Content

The Components

To find a W-Wing, you need three specific parts:

Two Identical Bi-Value Cells: Two cells containing the exact same pair of numbers, for example, {4, 7}. They do not need to be in the same house.

A Strong Link: A specific connection involving one of those numbers (e.g., the '4'). Recall: a Strong Link means "In this Row/Col/Box, the number '4' appears exactly twice."

The Pattern

Cell 1: {4, 7}

Cell 2: {4, 7}

The Bridge: A Strong Link on the number 4 connects these two cells (Cell 1 sees one end of the link, and Cell 2 sees the other end).

The Logic: "The See-Saw"

Consider the Strong Link on '4'. Since there are only two '4's in that unit, one of them must be true.

Scenario A: The left-side '4' is true. If the link is '4', then Cell 1 cannot be 4. It must be 7.

Scenario B: The right-side '4' is true. If the link is '4', then Cell 2 cannot be 4. It must be 7.

Conclusion: Since the Strong Link demands that one '4' exists, it forces at least one of our bi-value cells to become a 7. We don't know which one is the 7, but we know one of them is.

The Elimination

Because 7 must exist in Cell 1 or Cell 2:

Look for any cell that is seen by both Cell 1 and Cell 2.

Action: Eliminate the candidate 7 (the non-link number) from that intersection.

Visual Check

Spot two cells with the pair {A, B}.

Ask: "Can I connect them with an 'A' chain?"

If yes, kill 'B' from the crossfire.

3. Section Summary

The W-Wing is essentially a "disjointed Naked Pair." A normal Naked Pair requires the cells to be in the same house. A W-Wing allows the cells to be far apart, using a Strong Link as a "teleportation cable" to connect them. It is one of the most common techniques for breaking 7.0+ difficulty puzzles.
