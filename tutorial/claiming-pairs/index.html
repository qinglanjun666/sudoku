Claiming Pairs / Box-Line Reduction

1. What You Will Master

In this section, you will learn Claiming Pairs (also known as Box-Line Reduction). This is the inverse of the Pointing Pair. You will learn to recognize when a Row or Column forces a number into a specific Box, thereby eliminating that number from the rest of the Box. This logic is subtle but essential for cleaning up pencil marks in crowded grids.

2. Main Content

The Concept

A Claiming Pair occurs when a candidate appears multiple times in a Row (or Column), but all those instances fall within a single Box.

Because the number must exist somewhere in that Row, and it can only be inside that specific Box, the Row "claims" the right to place that number in the Box. Therefore, the number cannot exist anywhere else in that Box.

The Logic Step-by-Step

Let's look at Row 2.

Scan: You are looking for the number '7'.

Observation: In Row 2, the only places a '7' can go are R2C4 and R2C5.

Alignment: Notice that both R2C4 and R2C5 are inside Box 2 (top-middle).

Deduction:

The '7' of Row 2 must be in Box 2.

Therefore, the '7' for Box 2 must be one of these two cells.

Action: You can look at the rest of Box 2 (R1C4, R1C5, R3C4, R3C5, etc.) and eliminate any pencil marks for '7'.

Difference from Pointing Pairs

Pointing Pair: "The Box forces the Line." (We know it's in the box 鈫?eliminate from line).

Claiming Pair: "The Line forces the Box." (We know it's in the line 鈫?eliminate from the rest of the box).

Visual Tip

Claiming Pairs are easiest to spot when you are scanning a specific number across rows. If you see "Row 5 only has 3s here and here," check immediately if those spots share a box. If they do, you have a Claiming Pair.

3. Section Summary

Claiming Pairs / Box-Line Reduction is a powerful cleanup tool. It reinforces the idea that Rows and Boxes are interconnected. If a Row says "My '7' must be here," the Box must respect that and remove its own '7's from other locations. Mastering this, along with Pointing Pairs, completes your understanding of Intersection logic.
