Cross-Hatching

1. What You Will Master

In this section, you will learn Cross-Hatching, the most efficient visual technique for spotting Hidden Singles. Instead of randomly looking at numbers, you will learn a systematic method to scan the grid box-by-box. This is the primary skill that separates a "confused beginner" from a "competent solver."

2. Main Content

The Concept

Cross-Hatching is a systematic way of finding Hidden Singles. It involves focusing on one specific number (e.g., "9") and scanning all the 3×3 Boxes to see where that number is missing. You use the "laser beams" from existing 9s to draw imaginary lines across the grid, leaving only safe spaces behind.

The Workflow

To perform Cross-Hatching effectively, follow this routine:

  • Pick a Number: Start with the number 1.
  • Scan the Mega-Rows: Look at the top three boxes (Boxes 1, 2, 3) as a group.
  • Draw the Lines: If Box 1 has a '1' and Box 2 has a '1', look at Box 3. The '1's from Box 1 and 2 shoot lines across the rows of Box 3. Eliminate those rows.
  • Check Columns: Look for vertical '1's that slice through Box 3.
  • Place the Digit: If only one spot is left in Box 3, fill it in.
  • Repeat: Move to the middle boxes (4, 5, 6) and bottom boxes (7, 8, 9).
  • Cycle: Once you finish checking '1's, move to '2's, then '3's, all the way to '9'.

Why "Cycling" Works

Often, placing a '7' in the early game might unlock a '4' that was previously impossible to find. By cycling through numbers 1–9 repeatedly, you catch these new opportunities.

Visualizing the "T" and "L" Shapes

When scanning, you are often looking for intersections that form shapes:

  • The "T" Shape: A row and a column intersect to block 4 out of 5 cells in a box.
  • The "L" Shape: A row and column block the corner cells.

3. Section Summary

Cross-Hatching turns the chaos of 81 cells into a methodical process. By isolating one number at a time and scanning systematically (Box 1–2–3, then 4–5–6), you ensure you never miss an easy placement. Remember: Don't stare at the whole board. Pick a number, hunt it down, and then move to the next one.
