Remote Pairs

1. What You Will Master

In this section, you will learn Remote Pairs. This is a specific variation of the XY-Chain where every cell in the chain contains the exact same pair of numbers. You will learn how to use simple "Coloring" logic on these identical pairs to perform powerful eliminations across the grid.

2. Main Content

The Components

To find a Remote Pair, you need:

A Chain of Bi-Value Cells: A series of cells connected by weak links (sharing rows, columns, or boxes).

Identical Candidates: Every cell in the chain must contain the same two numbers (e.g., {4, 7}).

Even Length: The chain must consist of an even number of cells (4, 6, 8, etc.).

The Logic: "Parity"

Since every cell is {4, 7}, we don't need to track changing numbers like in an XY-Chain. We can just use A and B.

Trace a chain of four cells:

Cell 1 (Start): Suppose it is 4.

Cell 2: Must be 7 (because it sees Cell 1).

Cell 3: Must be 4 (because it sees Cell 2).

Cell 4 (End): Must be 7 (because it sees Cell 3).

Result: If Cell 1 is 4, Cell 4 is 7. If Cell 1 is 7, Cell 4 is 4. Cell 1 and Cell 4 are opposite values; one is 4 and the other is 7. We don't know which is which, but we know they differ.

The Elimination

Because Cell 1 and Cell 4 hold the values 4 and 7 between them:

Identify the Start and End of your chain (an even number of steps apart).

Find any cell that is seen by both the Start and the End.

Action: Eliminate both candidates {4, 7} from that intersection cell.

Reasoning: That cell sees a 4 (from one end) and a 7 (from the other end). It cannot be either.

Why "Even Number" Matters

If your chain has an odd number of cells (e.g., three): Cell 1 is A, Cell 2 is B, Cell 3 is A. Result: Cell 1 and Cell 3 are the same number. This recovers Two-String Kite / Skyscraper-style logic (contradiction checks) but does not give the "opposite value" guarantee needed for double elimination in Remote Pairs.

How to Spot It

Scan the grid for many cells with the pair {3, 8}. Pick one, jump to a neighbor with {3, 8}, then jump to another. Count your steps: 1, 2, 3, 4. Look at the 1st and 4th cell. Do they share a common neighbor? Remove {3, 8} from that neighbor.

3. Section Summary

Remote Pairs are the simplest introduction to Alternating Inference. You are essentially coloring the grid: "This is A, this is B, this is A, this is B." Any cell that sees an A and a B simultaneously is instantly cleared. It is a highly efficient technique found often in symmetrical puzzles.
