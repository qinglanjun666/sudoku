Finned X-Wing

1. What You Will Master

In this section, you will learn the Finned X-Wing. You will discover what to do when an X-Wing is almost perfect but has one or two extra candidates (the "Fin") spoiling the rectangle. You will master the logic of "Either/Or" to perform eliminations in the specific box where the Fin resides.

2. Main Content

The Concept

A Finned X-Wing is a normal X-Wing that has one small flaw: one of the Base Rows has an extra candidate (the Fin) that sits right next to one of the corners.

The Shape: Imagine a perfect X-Wing rectangle. Now, add one extra candidate in the same Row as the bottom-right corner, but inside the same Box as that corner.

The Problem: Because of this extra candidate, we cannot say the columns are "locked" anymore. The X-Wing might not be true.

The Logic: "Heads I Win, Tails You Lose"

The logic relies on checking two scenarios. Let's say the Fin is in the bottom-right corner.

Scenario A: The Fin is the True Number. If the Fin is true, any candidate in the same Box as the Fin is eliminated.

Scenario B: The Fin is False. If the Fin is false, then the remaining pattern IS a perfect X-Wing. Therefore, the X-Wing eliminates candidates in the Column (Cover Set).

The Intersection

So, where can we eliminate? We can only eliminate candidates that are destroyed in BOTH scenarios.

This means we look for cells that are seen by the X-Wing (the Column) AND seen by the Fin (the Box).

Conclusion: You can eliminate candidates in the specific corner where the Column and the Box overlap.

Step-by-Step

Find an Almost X-Wing: You see a rectangle, but one corner has a "neighbor" in the same box.

Identify the Fin: That neighbor is the Fin.

Target: Look at the cell that forms the "corner" of the X-Wing column and the Fin's box.

Fire: If that target cell contains the candidate, eliminate it.

3. Section Summary

The Finned X-Wing is a safety net. It tells you: "Even if this isn't a perfect X-Wing, it is powerful enough to kill candidates in this specific corner." It teaches you to look for the intersection of two truths. If a candidate dies whether the X-Wing is real OR the Fin is real, that candidate is dead for sure.
