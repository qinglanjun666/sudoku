Hidden Pairs

1. What You Will Master

In this section, you will learn to identify Hidden Pairs. Unlike Naked Pairs (which are easy to see because they are the only numbers in the cell), Hidden Pairs are buried under "noise." You will learn how to spot two specific numbers that share exclusive ownership of two cells, and how to use this knowledge to eliminate all other candidates from those cells.

2. Main Content

The Concept

A Hidden Pair occurs when two specific candidates (e.g., 6 and 9) appear in exactly two cells within a House, and nowhere else in that House.

Crucially, these two cells might also contain other candidates (like 1, 2, or 5).

Naked Pair: The cells contain only the pair.

Hidden Pair: The pair is buried among other numbers.

The Logic: "Exclusive Seats"

Imagine looking at Row 1.

You notice that the number 6 is only possible in R1C4 and R1C8.

You notice that the number 9 is also only possible in R1C4 and R1C8.

Conclusion: Since 6 and 9 must go in Row 1, and they only have these two seats available, they must take these seats.

R1C4 must be 6 or 9.

R1C8 must be 6 or 9.

The Elimination (The "Internal Clean-Up")

This is the key difference from Naked Subsets:

With Naked Pairs, you eliminate candidates from the neighbors.

With Hidden Pairs, you eliminate candidates from the cells themselves.

If R1C4 currently has candidates {1, 2, 6, 9}, but you know it is part of a Hidden Pair (6/9), you can safely delete the 1 and 2. They are intruders. The 6 and 9 have claimed the territory.

How to Spot Them

  • Scan by Number: Don't look at cells; look at specific digits. Ask: "Where are the 6s in this box? Only here and here."
  • Cross-Reference: "Are there any other numbers restricted to these exact same two spots?"
  • The Reveal: If you find a match, you have a Hidden Pair.

Why Use It?

Hidden Pairs essentially convert a messy cell into a clean Naked Pair. They strip away the noise and simplify the puzzle.

3. Section Summary

A Hidden Pair is a relationship of exclusivity. If you find two numbers that are "hiding" in the same two cells (and nowhere else in that row/column/box), they evict everyone else. The numbers say: "We are the only ones allowed in these two spots; everyone else get out." This action clears the cell of extra candidates, often revealing a Naked Pair that helps you solve the rest of the house.
