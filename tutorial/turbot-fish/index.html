Turbot Fish

1. What You Will Master

In this section, you will learn the Turbot Fish. This is not a new pattern, but rather the general theory behind the Skyscraper and the Two-String Kite. You will learn how to spot any chain of three Strong Links, regardless of its shape, and use it to eliminate candidates. Mastering this gives you the flexibility to find logic patterns that don't look like standard textbook shapes.

2. Main Content

The Concept

The Skyscraper and Two-String Kite are just specific shapes of a Turbot Fish.

A Turbot Fish is simply a chain of three Strong Links connected by Weak Links.

Definition: A single-digit chain involving exactly four segments (connecting five cells, though often simplified to focus on the links).

The Logic: "If not here, then there"

Trace the logic from the Start cell to the End cell:

Assume the Start cell is False (does not contain the number).

If Start is False 鈫?the next cell in the Strong Link must be True.

If that cell is True 鈫?it kills the next cell (Weak Link), making it False.

If that cell is False 鈫?the final cell in the next Strong Link must be True (the End).

Conclusion: If the Start is False, the End is True. Therefore, Start and End cannot both be False. One of them holds the number.

The Elimination

Because the number must exist in either the Start cell or the End cell:

Identify the two ends of your chain.

Find the Kill Zone: The intersection of the Start cell and the End cell.

Action: Eliminate the candidate from that intersection.

Why "Turbot Fish"?

It is named after a flatfish. The pattern is usually headless (no closed loop) and asymmetrical.

If the chain is parallel: it's a Skyscraper.

If the chain turns a corner: it's a Two-String Kite.

If the chain is random: it's a Turbot Fish.

How to Spot It

Don't look for a shape. Look for Conjugate Pairs (Strong Links).

Highlight a number (e.g., '5').

Find a Strong Link.

From one end, can you jump to another Strong Link via a shared house?

Does that lead to a third Strong Link?

If yes, check the ends. Do they see a common cell? Eliminate.
