Hidden Quads

1. What You Will Master

In this section, you will learn about Hidden Quads. This involves identifying four distinct numbers that are restricted to exactly four specific cells in a House. While finding these directly is a feat of mental gymnastics, you will learn the concept of "Inversion"—why it is often easier to look for the other 5 numbers than to find the hidden 4.

2. Main Content

The Concept

A Hidden Quad exists when four candidates (e.g., {1, 3, 5, 7}) appear only in four specific cells within a House.

These four cells may be filled with many other "noise" candidates. But because the 1, 3, 5, and 7 have nowhere else to go in that House, they claim these four cells for themselves.

The Logic

Condition: In Row 2, the numbers 1, 3, 5, and 7 only appear in cells A, B, C, and D.

Action: Eliminate all other candidates (the noise) from cells A, B, C, and D.

Result: The cells become a clean Naked Quad.

The Strategy of Inversion (The Naked Quint)

Let's be realistic: scanning for a Hidden Quad is incredibly hard. Your eyes have to track four numbers across a sea of pencil marks. There is a smarter way: Inversion.

In any House (9 cells):

If you have a Hidden Quad (4 cells), the remaining 5 cells must form a Naked Quint.

It is often easier to look at the 5 cells that don't have the magic numbers. Do those 5 cells only contain candidates from the other 5 digits?

If yes, you have effectively found the Quad by finding what it is not.

Example:

You suspect a Hidden Quad of {1, 2, 3, 4} in four cells.

Look at the other 5 cells. Do they contain any 1, 2, 3, or 4?

If the answer is No, then you have confirmed the Quad. The 1, 2, 3, and 4 are "trapped" in the first group.

3. Section Summary

Hidden Quads represent the limit of practical human scanning. While you may not spot them often, understanding their existence allows you to verify complex board states. Remember the principle of exclusivity: if four numbers have only four possible homes, they evict all other guests from those homes. If you struggle to see the Hidden Quad, try looking for the Naked Quint in the remaining cells—sometimes the negative space reveals the shape.
