Naked Quads

1. What You Will Master

In this section, you will learn to identify Naked Quads. This involves finding four cells in a single house that collectively share exactly four candidates. Mastering this is a badge of honor; detecting a "scrambled" Naked Quad is one of the most difficult visual tasks in the Apprentice Level, but it is devastatingly effective at breaking gridlocks.

2. Main Content

The Concept

A Naked Quad exists when four cells in the same House contain candidates from a pool of exactly four numbers. Just like triples, the cells do not need to contain all four numbers. They just need to contain nothing else but those four.

The Rule: 4 Cells = 4 Candidates.

The Result: Those 4 numbers are locked in those 4 cells. You can eliminate them from the other 5 cells in the House.

The "Messy" Reality

A Naked Quad rarely looks clean like 1234, 1234, 1234, 1234. It usually looks like a scattered mess.

Example Scenario: Imagine looking at Row 5. You see four cells with these pencil marks:

Cell A: 1, 2

Cell B: 2, 3, 4

Cell C: 1, 4

Cell D: 3, 4

At first glance, this looks random. But let's check the pool of numbers:

The only numbers appearing in these four cells are {1, 2, 3, 4}.

There are no 5s, 6s, 7s, etc., inside these cells.

Conclusion: This is a Naked Quad. The numbers 1, 2, 3, and 4 must settle into Cells A, B, C, and D.

The Elimination

Because {1, 2, 3, 4} are "taken" by this group, you look at the other five cells in Row 5. If you see a 1, 2, 3, or 4 pencil mark in those other cells, delete them instantly.

Pro Tip: Counting the "Outsiders"

Because spotting 4 specific cells is hard, it is sometimes easier to look at the other cells in the house.

If a Row has 9 cells, and you find a Naked Quad (4 cells), that means the other 5 cells form a "Hidden Quint."

Conversely, if you see 5 cells that act as a set, the remaining 4 must be a Naked Quad.

Advice: Always look for the smaller group. If 4 cells are confusing, check if the other 5 cells are easier to analyze (though 5 is usually harder!).

3. Section Summary

The Naked Quad is the heavyweight champion of subsets. It locks down nearly half a row or box. The key to spotting them is not to look for identical cells, but to look for a closed system. Ask yourself: "Do these four cells contain any digits outside of this group of four?" If the answer is No, you have found your Quad. Lock them in, and clean up the rest of the house.
