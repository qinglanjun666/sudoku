Unique Rectangle Type 1

1. What You Will Master

In this section, you will learn Unique Rectangle (UR) Type 1. You will learn to spot a "danger zone"—a rectangle of four cells where three corners contain identical pairs (e.g., {4, 9}) and the fourth corner contains those numbers plus extras. You will learn why you can instantly solve that fourth cell by "defusing" the Deadly Pattern.

2. Main Content

The Setup

You are scanning for a potential Deadly Pattern (4 cells, 2 boxes, forming a rectangle).

The Candidates: You notice a pair, say {4, 9}.

  • Corner 1: Contains exactly {4, 9}.
  • Corner 2: Contains exactly {4, 9}.
  • Corner 3: Contains exactly {4, 9}.
  • Corner 4: Contains {4, 9} ... plus extra numbers (e.g., {4, 9, 5}).

The Logic: "The Escape Hatch"

Look at Corner 4 (the cell with {4, 9, 5}).

Hypothesis: What happens if this cell is a 4 or a 9?

If it is 4 or 9, then ALL FOUR corners become the pair {4, 9}. This creates a Deadly Pattern (as learned in Section 15.1). This implies the puzzle has two solutions, which is impossible.

Conclusion: Therefore, Corner 4 CANNOT be a 4 and CANNOT be a 9.

Result: The only option left is the "extra" number. The cell MUST be 5.

The Elimination

  • Identify the 4-cell rectangle confined to two boxes.
  • Confirm that three of the corners are purely the "Deadly Pair" (Bi-Value Cells).
  • Look at the fourth corner.
  • Action: Eliminate the Deadly Pair numbers from that cell. Whatever is left is the answer.
  • If the cell was {4, 9, 5}, it becomes 5.
  • If the cell was {4, 9, 5, 8}, it becomes {5, 8}.

Example

Row 2: R2C3 {1, 6}, R2C7 {1, 6}

Row 5: R5C3 {1, 6}, R5C7 {1, 6, 8}

Check Boxes: R2C3 and R5C3 are in Box 4. R2C7 and R5C7 are in Box 6. (Valid 2-Box setup).

Logic: If R5C7 is 1 or 6, the puzzle breaks.

Solution: R5C7 is 8.

3. Section Summary

Unique Rectangle Type 1 is checking for "Three of a Kind." If you see three corners of a rectangle that are identical twins {A, B}, the fourth corner is under immense pressure. It is forbidden from being A or B. It must be something else. This logic essentially turns a multi-choice cell into a Naked Single.
