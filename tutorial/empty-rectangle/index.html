Empty Rectangle

1. What You Will Master

In this final section of Level 3, you will learn the Empty Rectangle (ER). This is a specialized intersection technique involving a single number. It is extremely common in computer-generated puzzles. You will learn how to spot a specific "L-Shape" of candidates within a box and use it to eliminate a candidate that is "pincered" by the box and an external link.

2. Main Content

The Components

To find an Empty Rectangle pattern, focus on one specific number (e.g., '7') and locate two connected components:

The ER Box: A 3x3 Box where the candidates for '7' are restricted to exactly one Row and one Column within that box. This forms a geometric L-shape (sometimes T-shape or Plus-shape) inside the box. The rest of the box鈥攖he "rectangle" area that is not part of the L鈥攊s empty of '7's.

The Conjugate Pair: A strong link (exactly two candidates) in a Row or Column elsewhere on the grid that connects to one arm of the L.

The Logic: The "Pincer" Attack

Imagine the ER Box is in the top-left (Box 1). The candidates form an L-shape in Row 1 and Column 1 of that box. Now, imagine a Conjugate Pair in Row 1 far to the right.

End A of the pair lies in Row 1 (connecting to the ER Box's row arm). End B of the pair lies in Column 8.

The Deduction

We verify two possible realities for the L-shape in Box 1:

Scenario A: The '7' is in Row 1 of Box 1. This eliminates the '7' at End A of the Conjugate Pair. Therefore, End B (in Column 8) must be the '7'.

Scenario B: The '7' is not in Row 1 of Box 1. Because of the L-shape restriction, the '7' must be in Column 1 of Box 1.

The Elimination

Now look for a cell that is destroyed by both scenarios. Find the cell where Column 1 (from Scenario B) intersects with Column 8 (from Scenario A's conjugate pair result). If there is a '7' in that intersection cell, it is impossible鈥攖his cell "sees" a '7' in either reality.

Action: Eliminate the '7' from that intersection.

Simplified Visual Check

Find the L: Look for a box where a number is confined to one Row and one Column.

Shoot the Laser: Draw a line out from one arm of the L.

Hit a Mirror: The line must hit a Strong Link (Conjugate Pair).

Reflect: Turn 90 degrees at the Strong Link.

The Kill Zone: Where does that reflected line intersect the other arm of the L? That is your elimination target.

3. Section Summary

The Empty Rectangle is the bridge between Box Logic and Line Logic. It essentially says: "If a number isn't in this row of the box, it must be in this column. By connecting that row to a switch (pair), I can create a pincer attack on a distant cell." It is the last Single Digit technique before you enter the complex world of Multi-Digit geometry in Level 4.
