Hidden Single

1. What You Will Master

In this section, you will master a fundamental candidate-based technique: the Hidden Single. A Hidden Single occurs when, within a single House (Row, Column, or Box), a particular digit can appear in only one candidate position. That position is logically forced and must be filled with that digit.

2. Main Content

The Concept

Consider a House and focus on a specific digit, such as 5. If, after considering all constraints, only one cell in that House can take the digit 5 as a candidate, then that cell is a Hidden Single for 5 and must be set to 5.

How to Spot It

  • List candidates: Note all possible digits for each empty cell in the House.
  • Scan by digit: Pick a digit (1–9) and see where it can legally go.
  • Find uniqueness: If a digit appears in exactly one cell’s candidate list within that House, that cell is forced.
  • Place the digit: Fill the cell with the unique digit and update peers accordingly.

Example

In Box 2 (top-middle 3×3), suppose the empty cells have candidates as follows:

{R1C5: 2,5}, {R2C4: 1,5,7}, {R2C6: 2,7}, {R3C5: 3,5}. The digit 3 appears only in {R3C5}. Therefore, R3C5 is a Hidden Single for 3 and must be set to 3.

Why It Matters

Hidden Singles reveal forced placements that may be obscured within candidate lists—hence “hidden.” Placing one Hidden Single often triggers cascading updates: peers lose candidates, new Full Houses appear, and additional Singles become visible.

3. Section Summary

A Hidden Single is the unique candidate for a digit within a House. Always scan by digits across Rows, Columns, and Boxes. When a digit has exactly one legal spot, place it with confidence.
