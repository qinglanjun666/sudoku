Sashimi X-Wing

1. What You Will Master

In this section, you will learn the Sashimi X-Wing. This is a variation of the Finned X-Wing where the "main" corner of the rectangle is actually missing (it does not contain the candidate), but the "Fin" is still there. You will learn to recognize that even without the perfect corner, the presence of the Fin allows the exact same logic to apply.

2. Main Content

The Concept

Recall the Finned X-Wing: A perfect rectangle + a Fin. A Sashimi X-Wing is: A rectangle with a missing corner + a Fin.

The Setup: You are looking for an X-Wing shape.

The Glitch: One of the four corners does not have the candidate.

The Save: However, there is a candidate right next to that missing corner (in the same Box) that acts as a "Fin."

Effectively: The "Fin" has slid over to replace the corner.

The Logic

The logic is identical to the Finned X-Wing. We look at the "Fin" (the candidate inside the box).

Scenario A: The Fin is True. If the Fin is the solution, we eliminate candidates in its Box (specifically, the target cell).

Scenario B: The Fin is False. If the Fin is false, where must the number go?

Because the corner is missing, the number must be at the other end of that row. This forces the other column to activate, which forces the top corner... which eliminates the target.

The Target Zone

The elimination target is the same: The intersection of the Fin's Box and the X-Wing's Column. If a candidate sits in a cell that is "seen" by the Fin and "seen" by the vertical column of the X-Wing, it can be eliminated.

Why Distinguish Sashimi vs. Finned?

Technically, you don't need to distinguish them. Many pro players just call them both "Finned X-Wings." The only reason we use the term Sashimi is to help you spot them: sometimes your eyes miss the pattern because the rectangle isn't closed. Knowing that "broken rectangles work too" is a powerful psychological tool.

3. Section Summary

The Sashimi X-Wing teaches you that geometric perfection isn't required for logic. If a candidate in a box is the only alternative to an X-Wing structure, it acts as a connected part of that structure. Whether the corner cell is there (Finned) or missing (Sashimi), the logic remains: "Either the Fin is true, or the X-Wing is true. Both kill the common enemy."
