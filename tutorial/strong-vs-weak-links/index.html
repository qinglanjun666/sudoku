Strong vs. Weak Links

1. What You Will Master

In this section, you will learn the fundamental physics of Sudoku: Strong Links and Weak Links. These are the building blocks of every advanced chain (AIC, Forcing Chains, etc.). You will understand the difference between "If A is False, B is True" and "If A is True, B is False," and why distinguishing them is critical for solving Master-level puzzles.

2. Main Content

The Concept

All Sudoku chains are based on alternating ON and OFF states. To connect these states, we use links.

1. The Weak Link (The "Killer")

Definition: Two candidates are weakly linked if they cannot both be TRUE.

Condition: They simply see each other (share a Row, Column, or Box).

Logic: If Cell A is ON (True), then Cell B must be OFF (False).

Note: If Cell A is OFF, we know nothing about Cell B. It could be ON or OFF.

Symbol: Often drawn as a dashed line (A - B).

2. The Strong Link (The "Savior")

Definition: Two candidates are strongly linked if they cannot both be FALSE.

Condition: They are the only two candidates for a specific number in a House (Row, Column, or Box).

Logic: If Cell A is OFF (False), then Cell B must be ON (True).

Note: This is the powerful link. It forces a number into existence.

Symbol: Often drawn as a solid or double line (A = B).

The Hierarchy

A Strong Link is also a Weak Link. If there are only two candidates in a house (Strong), and you turn one ON, the other must turn OFF. Therefore, you can use a Strong Link as a Weak Link whenever you want.

A Weak Link is not necessarily a Strong Link. If there are three candidates in a house, they are all weakly linked to each other. But if you turn one OFF, you don't know which of the others is ON.

Why Alternating Matters

To build a chain (like an X-Wing or XY-Chain), you must alternate between these states to create a logical flow:

If you try to chain two Weak Links together ("If A is OFF, B is unknown... and if B is unknown, C is unknown..."), the chain breaks. You must bridge gaps using Strong Links.

Visual Identification

Strong: Look for the number 2. Two candidates in a row, two candidates in a box, or a bi-value cell.

Weak: Look for proximity. Any two candidates sharing a house.

3. Section Summary

Think of Strong Links as the engine and Weak Links as the transmission. Strong Links generate truth ("This must be true"). Weak Links transmit consequences ("Since this is true, that must be false"). In the upcoming chapters, we will construct Alternating Inference Chains (AIC). The rule will always be: Strong, Weak, Strong, Weak. Mastering this rhythm is the secret to becoming a Sudoku Master.
