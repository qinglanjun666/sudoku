Skyscraper

1. What You Will Master

In this section, you will learn the Skyscraper. You can think of this as a "Mutated X-Wing." You will learn to identify two rows (or columns) that have a strong link on a specific number, where one side lines up perfectly and the other side is staggered. You will master the logic of eliminating candidates that are caught in the crossfire of the staggered "roof."

2. Main Content

The Concept

A Skyscraper is formed by a specific digit (e.g., '6') appearing in two Base lines (let's say two Rows). In each Row, the '6' appears exactly twice (a Strong Link).

The Foundation: Two of the candidates are in the same Column (they align vertically).

The Roof: The other two candidates are in different Columns (they are staggered).

If you connect the dots, it doesn't form a rectangle (X-Wing). Instead, it looks like two tall towers of different heights鈥攁 Skyscraper.

The Logic: "The Tilted Roof"

Consider the two staggered tops of the Skyscraper (the unaligned cells). Call them Top A and Top B. Because of the strong links in the rows and the alignment in the column:

If Top A is False: The number must be in the Foundation of that row.

The Foundation Link: Since the number is in the foundation of Row 1, it cannot be in the foundation of Row 2 (they share a column).

Result: Therefore, the number must be in Top B.

Conclusion: At least one of the tops (Top A or Top B) must contain the number. They cannot both be false.

The Elimination

Because the number exists in Top A or Top B:

Identify the two staggered cells (the tips of the towers).

Find the Kill Zone: Any cell that is seen by both Top A and Top B.

Action: Eliminate the candidate from that zone.

Example

Row 2: The digit is in R2C1 and R2C5.

Row 6: The digit is in R6C1 and R6C8.

The Check:

Column 1 aligns perfectly (The Foundation).

Column 5 and Column 8 are staggered (The Roof).

The Action: Look for a cell that sees both R2C5 and R6C8. Example: R2C8 sees both. R6C5 sees both. Delete the digit from those cells.

3. Section Summary

The Skyscraper is the first step in understanding that you don't need perfect rectangles to solve Sudoku. As long as one side of the pattern is solid (aligned), the other side can be loose. The logic shifts from eliminating the line (X-Wing) to eliminating the intersection of the loose ends (Skyscraper).
