XY-Chain

1. What You Will Master

In this section, you will learn the XY-Chain. You will discover how to connect a series of Bi-Value Cells (cells with exactly two notes) into a long snake-like structure. You will learn to prove that the cell at the head of the snake and the cell at the tail forces a specific number to be true, eliminating that number from any cell that sees both ends.

2. Main Content

The Concept

Recall the XY-Wing (Section 11.1): it had three cells. An XY-Chain is simply an XY-Wing that has been stretched out. It can have 4, 5, 6, or even 20 cells.

Requirement: Every link in the chain must involve Bi-Value Cells.

The Logic: Use the internal strong link of each cell. If a cell has notes {1, 2}, and we say "It is not 1," then it must be 2. This certainty allows us to move to the next cell.

The Structure

Imagine a chain of cells:

Start: {1, 2}

Step 2: {2, 3} (sees Start)

Step 3: {3, 4} (sees Step 2)

End: {4, 1} (sees Step 3)

We started with 1 and ended with 1.

The Logic Trace

Test the Start cell {1, 2}:

Option A: The Start cell is 1.

Option B: The Start cell is not 1 → it must be 2 → Step 2 cannot be 2 → Step 2 must be 3 → Step 3 cannot be 3 → Step 3 must be 4 → End cannot be 4 → End must be 1.

Conclusion: Whether Option A or B, the number 1 appears at either the Start or the End. The digit is locked at the ends of the chain.

The Elimination

Because the digit (e.g., '1') must exist in the Start or the End:

Identify any cell that is seen by both endpoints.

Action: Eliminate the candidate '1' from that intersection.

Note: The intermediate steps (2, 3, 4) are connectors. The elimination only happens for the digit that starts and ends the chain.

How to Spot It

Scan for Bi-Value Cells (BVCs). Pick a BVC and a number, e.g., "If this isn't 1, it's 2." Follow the 2 to a neighbor {2, 5} → that neighbor becomes 5. Continue following until a cell brings you back to your original number '1'. Then check the intersection of Start and End.

3. Section Summary

The XY-Chain is a powerful weapon for cleaning up late-game puzzles. When the board is full of cells with only two options, an XY-Chain is almost guaranteed to exist. It relies on the simple binary logic of "If not this, then that," repeated until it forces a lock at the ends.
