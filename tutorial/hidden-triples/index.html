Hidden Triples

1. What You Will Master

In this section, you will learn to identify Hidden Triples. You will practice the skill of spotting three specific numbers that are restricted to exactly three cells within a house. You will learn how to confidently "sweep out" the garbage candidates from these three cells, transforming a cluttered area into a solved logical unit.

2. Main Content

The Concept

A Hidden Triple occurs when three specific candidates (e.g., {2, 5, 8}) appear in exactly three cells within a House (Row, Column, or Box) and nowhere else in that House.

Just like the Hidden Pair, these cells are often filled with "noise" (random other candidates like 1, 3, or 7). Your goal is to identify that the 2, 5, and 8 own these three cells exclusively.

The "Incomplete" Hidden Triple

This is the tricky part: The three cells do not need to contain all three numbers each. They just need to be the only places where those numbers exist.

Example in a Box:

Cell A: {2, 5, 1, 9} (Contains 2, 5... plus noise)

Cell B: {5, 8, 3, 7} (Contains 5, 8... plus noise)

Cell C: {2, 8, 4, 6} (Contains 2, 8... plus noise)

Check the House: Are there any 2s, 5s, or 8s in the other 6 cells of the box?

If No: Then 2, 5, and 8 are Locked in Cells A, B, and C.

The Elimination

Once you confirm the Hidden Triple:

  • You keep the 2, 5, and 8.
  • You delete the noise (1, 9, 3, 7, 4, 6) from Cells A, B, and C.

Result: You are left with a clean Naked Triple ({2,5}, {5,8}, {2,8}).

Why They Are Hard to Find

Hidden Triples are camouflaged. You usually find them when you are looking for something else.

Pro Tip: If you see a House with lots of empty cells but only three spots where a few specific large numbers (like 7, 8, 9) can go, pause and check. Are those high numbers forming a Hidden Triple?

The Logic Check

Always ask: "Can the 2 go anywhere else?" "Can the 5 go anywhere else?" "Can the 8 go anywhere else?" If the answer to all three is "No, only in these three spots," then you have permission to clear the rest.

3. Section Summary

Hidden Triples act like a secret club. Three numbers have decided to occupy three specific seats. Even if those seats currently look crowded with other options, the "Club Members" (the triple) have priority rights. By recognizing this pattern, you can evict the non-members (the noise candidates), simplifying the grid significantly. This is usually the tipping point in solving complex "Hard" level puzzles.
