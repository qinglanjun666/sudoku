Rows, Columns, and Boxes

1. What You Will Master

In this section, you will learn the standard coordinate system used by Sudoku solvers and logic communities worldwide. You will master how to identify specific Rows, Columns, and Boxes by number, and how to pinpoint any individual cell using the "RxCy" notation. This is the essential "map reading" skill required for all future tutorials.

2. Main Content

The Coordinate System

To discuss logic, we need a way to point to specific squares. Just like a chessboard or a spreadsheet, the Sudoku grid follows a strict numbering system. We count everything starting from the top-left corner.

1. Rows (Horizontal)

There are 9 Rows in the grid. They are numbered 1 to 9, counting from top to bottom.

Row 1: The very top row.

Row 9: The very bottom row.

2. Columns (Vertical)

There are 9 Columns in the grid. They are numbered 1 to 9, counting from left to right.

Column 1: The far-left column.

Column 9: The far-right column.

3. Boxes (3脳3 Sub-grids)

The 9 Boxes are also numbered 1 to 9, following standard "reading order" (left to right, then top to bottom).

Box 1: Top-left corner.

Box 5: The center box (the heart of the grid).

Box 9: Bottom-right corner.

Cell Coordinates: The "RxCy" Notation

When we combine the Row number and Column number, we get a precise address for any cell. The standard notation is R[Row Number]C[Column Number].

R1C1: The top-left cell (Row 1, Column 1).

R5C5: The exact center cell.

R9C9: The bottom-right cell.

Example: If a tutorial says, "Look for a 7 in R3C2," you should look at the cell where the 3rd Row and the 2nd Column intersect.
