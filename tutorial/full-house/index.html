Full House (The Last Digit)

1. What You Will Master

In this section, you will learn the absolute easiest move in Sudoku: the Full House. You will master the habit of scanning the grid for Rows, Columns, or Boxes that are almost finished. This technique requires zero complex logic—just simple counting—and it is the fastest way to place numbers on the board.

2. Main Content

The Concept

A "Full House" occurs when any House (Row, Column, or Box) has exactly 8 digits filled in and only 1 empty cell remaining. Because of the rule that every house must contain numbers 1 through 9, the missing number is mathematically forced. There is no ambiguity.

How to Solve It

  • Scan: Look for a line or box that is crowded with numbers.
  • Count: Count the numbers present from 1 to 9.
  • Identify: Find the one number that is missing.
  • Fill: Place that missing number in the empty cell.

Example

Imagine Row 1 contains the following numbers: {1, 2, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9}. The cell at R1C3 is empty.

Analysis: We have almost everything. The only number missing from the set 1–9 is 3.

Action: R1C3 must be 3. It cannot be anything else.

Why It Matters

Advanced players often overlook this because they are too busy looking for complex patterns. However, "cleaning up" Full Houses is critical because placing that one number often creates new Full Houses in intersecting rows or columns, causing a chain reaction of easy solves.

3. Section Summary

The Full House is the "low-hanging fruit" of Sudoku. Whenever you see a Row, Column, or Box with only one empty spot, stop everything else and fill it immediately. It is the only time in the game where you don't need to look at the surrounding neighbors—the answer lies entirely within that single House.
