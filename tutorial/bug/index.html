BUG (Bivalue Universal Grave)

1. What You Will Master

In this section, you will learn the Bivalue Universal Grave (BUG) principle, specifically the BUG+1 technique. You will learn to recognize a board state where every remaining cell has exactly two candidates, except for one cell which has three. You will learn the simple counting trick to instantly solve that three-candidate cell.

2. Main Content

The Theory: The "Grave"

Imagine a Sudoku board where every single unsolved cell contains exactly two candidates (a Bi-Value Cell). If this state existed, the puzzle would be logically "dead." It would be a giant web of Deadly Patterns, meaning the puzzle would have either zero solutions or two solutions. Since valid Sudoku puzzles must have exactly one solution, a perfect BUG state is impossible.

The Situation: BUG+1

In real games, you often reach a state that is almost a BUG.

  • The Grid: The puzzle is mostly solved.
  • The Cells: Every empty cell has pencil marks.
  • The Pattern: Every single empty cell has exactly 2 candidates...
  • The Exception: ...except for ONE cell, which has 3 candidates.

This is called a BUG+1. The "+1" is the extra candidate in that tri-value cell. That extra candidate is the only thing keeping the puzzle alive. If you removed it, the puzzle would die (become invalid). Therefore, that extra candidate MUST be the solution.

The Logic: How to Find the "Extra"

You have found the tri-value cell (e.g., {2, 4, 7}). How do you know which number is the "extra" one that prevents the Grave?

The Shortcut Rule (The Counting Trick): Look at the Row (or Column, or Box) containing the BUG cell. Count how many times each candidate appears in that house.

  • Candidate '2': Appears 2 times total (once in our cell, once elsewhere).
  • Candidate '4': Appears 2 times total.
  • Candidate '7': Appears 3 times total.

Conclusion: The candidate that appears 3 times is the anomaly. It is the "+1". Action: The cell MUST be 7.

Why this works: In a perfect BUG (Deadly State), every candidate appears exactly twice in every house (forming closed loops). By finding the candidate that appears 3 times, you have found the "Odd Man Out" that breaks the loop and fixes the puzzle.

Step-by-Step Strategy

  • Endgame Scan: You are stuck at the end of a hard puzzle.
  • Bi-Value Check: Scan the empty cells. Are they all pairs? ({1,2}, {3,5}, {1,5}...).
  • Find the Pivot: Find the one cell that has 3 numbers.
  • Count: Look at that cell's Row. Count the instances of its numbers.
  • Solve: Two of the numbers will have a "buddy" (2 instances). One will be the "boss" (3 instances). Pick the boss.

3. Section Summary

The BUG+1 is the ultimate "Meta-Logic" move. It relies entirely on the assumption that the puzzle is valid. If you see a sea of Bi-Value cells, don't panic. Look for the island (the tri-value cell). That island holds the key. Solving it usually collapses the entire rest of the puzzle instantly.
