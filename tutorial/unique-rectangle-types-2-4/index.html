Unique Rectangle Types 2鈥?

1. What You Will Master

In this section, you will learn how to handle Unique Rectangles where two of the corners have extra candidates. You will master Type 2 (the most common variation), which creates a Strong Link, and briefly cover Type 3 (Subsets) and Type 4 (House Locks). These techniques turn a potential "Deadly Pattern" into a weapon for elimination.

2. Main Content

Recap: The Deadly Pattern

We are trying to avoid a state where four cells (in 2 boxes) form a rectangle with exactly candidates {A, B}. If this happens, the puzzle is broken.

Unique Rectangle Type 2: "The Floor"

The Setup: Top corners: {3, 8} (Clean). Bottom corners: {3, 8, 5} and {3, 8, 5} (Dirty).

The Logic: Look at the bottom row. One of these two cells MUST be 5. If neither is 5, then both become {3, 8}, forming the deadly rectangle. To prevent this, the 5 must exist in one of those two spots.

The Elimination: Since the 5 is locked into one of the two bottom cells, eliminate 5 from any other cell that sees BOTH of them (often clears 5s from the rest of that row or box).

Unique Rectangle Type 3: "The Subset"

The Setup: Top corners: {4, 9}. Bottom Left: {4, 9, 2}. Bottom Right: {4, 9, 7}. Extras are 2 and 7.

The Logic: As with Type 2, one of the bottom cells must be its extra digit (2 or 7). The two bottom cells act like a virtual pair {2, 7}. If other cells in the same house also contain {2, 7}, treat the UR cells as part of a Naked Triple or Quad.

The Elimination: Use subset logic: the bottom cells and neighbors form a subset that locks {2, 7}, allowing eliminations on peers.

Unique Rectangle Type 4: "The Lock"

The Setup: Standard rectangle. In one house (e.g., Row 9), the candidate '3' only exists inside the UR cells.

The Logic: Since '3' has nowhere else to go in the house, one UR cell MUST be 3. If a cell is 3, it cannot be 8. The ambiguity of the deadly pattern is impossible because the 3 is forced by the house rules.

The Elimination: Because the 3 is locked in, the other deadly digit (8) can be removed from those specific UR cells. Rare but powerful.

3. Section Summary

  • Type 1: 1 dirty corner 鈫?Solve that corner.
  • Type 2: 2 dirty corners (same extra) 鈫?The extra is locked in those two. Kill peers.
