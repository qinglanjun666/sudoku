Naked Triples

1. What You Will Master

In this section, you will learn to identify Naked Triples. This is the expanded version of the Naked Pair logic. You will master the ability to spot three cells that effectively "monopolize" three specific numbers. Crucially, you will learn to identify the tricky "incomplete" triples (e.g., [1,2], [2,3], [1,3]) that are often missed by novice players.

2. Main Content

The Concept

A Naked Triple occurs when three cells in the same House contain candidates from a set of exactly three numbers. Just like the pair, these three cells form a closed group. No other cell in that House can contain any of those three numbers.

The Easy Type: Perfect Triples

The easiest triple to spot is when all three cells look exactly the same.

Cell A: 1, 2, 3

Cell B: 1, 2, 3

Cell C: 1, 2, 3

Logic: Since 1, 2, and 3 must go into A, B, and C, they cannot be anywhere else in that row/column/box.

The Hard Type: Incomplete Triples

This is where most players get stuck. A Naked Triple is valid as long as the combined candidates of the three cells are limited to three numbers. They don't have to each have all three.

Example:

Cell A: 1, 2 (Only 1 or 2)

Cell B: 2, 3 (Only 2 or 3)

Cell C: 1, 3 (Only 1 or 3)

Analysis:

List all candidates present in these three cells: {1, 2, 3}.

Count of Candidates: 3.

Count of Cells: 3.

Conclusion: This is a Locked Triple. The numbers 1, 2, and 3 must inhabit Cells A, B, and C. We don't know the order yet, but we know they are trapped there.

Action: You can eliminate 1, 2, and 3 from any other cell in that House.

Step-by-Step Detection

  • Scan for small clusters: Look for cells with only 2 or 3 candidates.
  • Test the Group: Pick three cells. Add up all their candidates.
  • The Golden Ratio: Does the total number of unique candidates equal 3?

3. Section Summary

A Naked Triple is a "Three-for-Three" deal. Whether the cells look identical (123, 123, 123) or scrambled (12, 23, 13), the logic is the same: if three cells are restricted to share only three distinct numbers, those numbers are locked. Identifying these sets allows you to clear out "noise" candidates from the rest of the group, often revealing Naked Singles that were previously hidden.
